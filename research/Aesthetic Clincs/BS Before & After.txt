## 1. PERSONA PROFILE

**Meet: Dr. Sarah Chen, 38**  
Owner & Medical Director, Radiance Aesthetics & Wellness  
Location: Scottsdale, Arizona  
Revenue: $180k–$220k/month (fluctuates seasonally)  
Staff: 2 Nurse Injectors, 1 Esthetician, 2 Front Desk/Patient Coordinators, 1 Part-Time Marketing Coordinator

### Background & Context

Sarah opened Radiance five years ago after working as a Family Medicine physician who "fell in love with aesthetics." She's board-certified, passionate about patient transformations, and obsessed with building a premium brand. She invested heavily in Instagram ads, a beautiful space, top-tier equipment (laser, RF microneedling, CoolSculpting), and hired experienced injectors.

**Current Reality:**

- Spending $8k–$12k/month on Facebook/Instagram ads targeting Botox, filler, laser, and "mommy makeover" packages.
- Generating 80–150 leads/month, but **conversion to booked consults is around 35–40%**, far below the 60–70% she sees competitors claim.
- Front desk (Jessica and Maria) are constantly "in the weeds"—juggling walk-ins, checkout, phone calls, DMs, and Instagram lead forms.
- No-show rate for consults hovers around 25–30%; for follow-up treatments it's closer to 15–20%.
- She knows there's $400k+ in unscheduled treatment plans sitting in Aesthetic Record (her EMR), but no one has time to systematically call those patients back.

**Tools Stack:**

- **EMR/PMS:** Aesthetic Record (booking, charting, photos, consent forms)
- **Marketing/Ads:** Run by an agency (Meta, Google LSAs)
- **CRM:** GoHighLevel (set up by the agency but underutilized; staff rarely log into it)
- **Communication:** Texting from personal phones, Instagram DMs via the practice IG account (3 people share the login), front desk uses desk phone
- **Payments:** Square + Aesthetic Record integration
- **Reminders:** Manual texts from front desk the day before appointments

**Family & Obligations:**

- Married, two kids (ages 6 and 9)
- Husband works full-time in tech; they share school pickups but Sarah carries mental load for household
- Tries to be home by 6:30 pm for dinner 4 nights/week; often brings laptop home to "catch up" after kids are in bed

**Self-Image & Aspirations:**

- Sees herself as a **doctor who elevates lives**, not just a "business owner."
- Dreams of scaling to 2–3 locations, becoming a local authority (speaking at events, teaching injector courses).
- Frustrated that she's "stuck in the weeds" of operations instead of focusing on patient care, training staff, and growth strategy.
- Fears being seen as "just another med spa" in a saturated market.

## 4. DECISION POINTS & FAILURE LOOPS

### **Decision Points (Before AI)**

1. **"Do I respond to DMs myself, or hope someone on the team checks?"**
   - Usually: Sarah responds sporadically, team doesn't check = leads die
2. **"Do I hire another front desk person, or just accept we're maxed out?"**
   - Usually: Accept status quo, blame "bad leads"
3. **"Do I manually call the $380k unscheduled treatment list, or keep putting it off?"**
   - Usually: Keep putting it off
4. **"Do I invest in another CRM/tool, or is it just more tech debt?"**
   - Usually: Skepticism wins, stays stuck

### **Decision Points (After AI)**

1. **"Do I trust the AI to handle leads, or insist on reviewing every conversation?"**
   - Initially: Sarah reviews transcripts obsessively
   - After 2 weeks: Spot-checks, trusts the system
2. **"Do I let AI book directly, or require human approval?"**
   - Initially: Human approval required
   - After 1 month: AI books directly for standard consults; humans handle high-ticket only
3. **"Do I expand ad spend now that conversion is higher?"**
   - Sarah increases budget by 30% because she's confident leads won't be wasted

### **Failure Loops (Before AI)**

- **Loop 1:** Miss calls → Leads ghost → Blame "bad leads" → Keep missing calls
- **Loop 2:** Front desk overwhelmed → Skip follow-up → Revenue leaks → Hire more people → Still overwhelmed
- **Loop 3:** Buy CRM → Team doesn't adopt → CRM sits unused → Distrust new tools → Stay manual

### **Success Loops (After AI)**

- **Loop 1:** AI responds fast → More consults booked → Higher revenue → Trust system more → Expand marketing
- **Loop 2:** No-shows drop → Schedule fills predictably → Staff less stressed → Better patient experience → More referrals and repeat
- **Loop 3:** Reactivation automated → Dormant treatment converts → Revenue without ad spend → Confidence in database mining
