**Goal:** In 90 days, fix the three biggest operational leaks without stepping into diagnosis or clinical decision‑making territory.

### 3.1 SYSTEM 1 – AI FRONT DESK \& LEAD ROUTER

**Objective:** Capture and triage all inbound interest (phone, DMs, web) instantly and consistently, so no lead dies in the cracks.

**What we build:**

**Multi‑channel AI receptionist layer (no diagnosis):**

- **Channels covered:**
    - Website chat widget.[^13][^11][^1]
    - Instagram DM integration (via API or platform).[^11][^13][^1]
    - Phone overflow / AI receptionist (via VOIP integration or call forwarding).[^13][^11][^1]
- **AI capabilities (strictly limited to pre‑approved FAQs):**
    - Answer fixed questions only:[^31][^11][^13][^1]
        - Services offered.
        - Starting price ranges (broad bands, not exact quotes).
        - Generic downtime ranges ("most patients need X–Y days").
        - Hours, location, parking, "how to book."
        - Basic insurance script ("We're cash‑pay for cosmetic; here's how to discuss with your provider if medical").
    - If question is outside script (e.g., "Is this medication safe for me?"), AI responds: "That's something our clinical team needs to answer directly. Here's how to reach them / we'll have someone call you."[^1]
- **Lead intake:**
    - Collects: name, phone, email, main concern/treatment interest, rough timing ("ASAP," "next month," "just exploring").[^12][^11][^13][^1]
    - Pushes everything into **unified lead inbox** (dashboard).

**Unified lead inbox + scoring:**

- **Aggregates:** web forms, bot conversations, DMs, call logs into one view.[^12][^11][^13][^1]
- **Auto‑tags each lead:**
    - Source: call / IG DM / FB / web form / walk‑in.[^1]
    - Intent: consult request vs "just pricing."[^1]
    - Likely service category: surgical (face/body/breast) vs medspa (injectables/lasers/skin).[^1]
    - Temperature: hot ("book me now"), warm ("interested, want to learn more"), cold ("just browsing").[^1]
- **Routing logic:**
    - **High‑value patterns** (e.g., mentions of "mommy makeover," "facelift," "out of town," "consult") → auto‑assigned to coordinators with response SLA (e.g., "respond within 30–60 min during business hours").[^12][^11][^13][^1]
    - **Low‑value / info‑only queries** → handled fully by bot or queued as "low priority" for staff to batch‑process.[^1]

**Real‑world behavior (mapped to their day):**

- **Weekend/evening DMs (the "after‑hours black hole"):**[^1]
    - AI responds within ~60 seconds with brand‑matched tone, answers FAQ, and either books or moves to nurture sequence.
    - Monday morning: owner/staff see dashboard summary: "4 new leads captured over the weekend. 2 consults booked, 2 in nurture."[^1]
- **Peak‑hour call overflow (the "lip filler" example):**[^1]
    - Front desk is checking out a patient; phone rings.
    - AI answers: "Hi, this is [Clinic]. How can I help you?"
    - Caller: "Do you do lip filler? How much?"
    - AI: explains price range, collects name/contact, offers booking link or "We'll have someone call you back within the hour."
    - Lead logged with full transcript, tagged "medspa – lip filler – warm," appears in coordinator queue.[^11][^13][^1]

**Pain directly solved:**

- No more after‑hours leads sitting unanswered until someone "remembers to check."[^1]
- No more missed peak‑hour calls because front desk is juggling 3 tasks.[^1]
- No more "I think we got 8 calls today" ambiguity → clear dashboard with lead count, source, and status.[^1]
- Owner stops manually triaging DMs and voicemails at night and during commute.[^1]

**Why it's low‑compliance:**

- AI only uses generic, non‑medical info; no symptoms, no treatment recommendations.[^15][^14][^12][^13][^11]
- All PHI (names, contact details, specific treatment history) is stored in their EMR/CRM (Aesthetic Record, GoHighLevel, etc.), which already handle HIPAA; your AI is the orchestration/routing layer only.[^14][^15][^12][^13][^11][^1]

### 3.2 SYSTEM 2 – SMART INTAKE \& PROVIDER BRIEFS

**Objective:** Improve consult→treatment conversion and reduce wasted slots by ensuring fit and prep before patient arrives.

**What we build:**

**Pre‑consult smart intake (online form or SMS link):**

Sent when consult is booked; completed before they arrive:[^1]

- **Questions (plain language, no jargon):**
    - "What bothers you most when you see yourself in photos or the mirror?"
    - "How soon do you want to see a change?" (ASAP / 1–3 months / 6+ months / just exploring)
    - "How much downtime can you tolerate?" (none / a few days / 1–2 weeks / longer is fine)
    - "Budget range for this?" (low / medium / high – no exact \$ required)
    - "Are you open to surgical options, or prefer non‑surgical only?" (yes / no / not sure / want to discuss)
    - "Have you had similar treatments or surgeries before?" (free text)

**AI scoring \& brief generation:**[^26][^32][^24][^25]

- **Lead score: A / B / C**
    - Weighting factors:
        - Clarity and realism of goal.
        - Budget band vs typical case cost for their concern.
        - Downtime tolerance vs realistic needs for effective treatment.
        - Surgery openness vs clinic's typical pathway for that concern.
- **Provider brief (one‑pager visible in EMR or dashboard before consult):**
    - **Top 3 things they want changed** (goals in their own words, verbatim where possible).[^24][^25][^26][^1]
    - **Likely blockers/fears:**
        - Scars, "looking fake," aggressive change, safety, downtime, cost sensitivity.[^26][^10][^24][^1]
    - **Context snapshot:**
        - New vs existing patient, referred vs ad vs event, prior treatments/surgeries.[^1]
    - **Pathway signal:** likely surgical candidate vs medspa‑only based on answers and clinic norms.[^1]

**Workflow:**

- Brief appears in provider's dashboard or can be printed before consult.[^1]
- Provider can override score and add human notes, which train future AI scoring.[^32][^24][^26]

**Real‑world impact (from their pain):**

- **Before:** Provider walks in cold, spends first 10 minutes fishing with "So, what brings you in today?"[^1]
- **After:** Provider walks in knowing: "This patient is worried about looking 'overdone,' has a tight timeline, and is cost‑sensitive. I can address all three in the first 2 minutes."[^1]
- **Before:** 35–40% of leads convert to consults; no way to tell which are serious vs tire‑kickers before they book.[^1]
- **After:** A‑score leads get prioritized; C‑score leads can be nurtured with education or invited to events instead of burning prime consult slots.[^1]

**Pain directly solved:**

- Fewer wasted consults on misaligned or low‑intent leads.[^1]
- Higher consult→treatment conversion because provider is prepared and patient feels "heard" immediately.[^25][^24][^26][^1]
- Owner's question "Are the leads trash or are we dropping the ball?" finally gets answered with data.[^1]

**Why it's low‑compliance:**

- AI summarizes patient‑provided preferences and history only; it does not recommend or alter treatments.[^33][^24][^26]
- Provider still makes all clinical decisions and owns charting.[^33][^24][^26][^1]
- Data lives within their EMR/CRM; your AI is a summarizer, not a clinical decision support tool.[^24][^26][^33][^1]

### 3.3 SYSTEM 3 – REACTIVATION \& EDUCATION ENGINE

**Objective:** Systematically turn dormant treatment plans and one‑off visits into booked revenue, while calming patient anxiety with proactive education.

**What we build:**

**Reactivation flows (the "\$380k unscheduled treatment" problem):**[^1]

- **Data pull:**
    - Connects to Aesthetic Record (or their EMR) via API to pull unscheduled treatment plans and past patient records.[^32][^11][^1]
- **Segmentation:**
    - By treatment value (prioritize \$5k+ plans first).
    - By time elapsed (e.g., consult was 1–3 months ago, treatment package started but not completed).
    - By treatment type (surgery vs injectables vs lasers).[^1]
- **Automated outreach sequences:**
    - Personalized SMS/email:
        - "Hi [Name], you had a plan for [treatment category] we discussed back in [month]. Most patients who start this see best results when they complete within X timeframe. Want to find a time that works?"[^32][^11][^1]
    - AI handles conversational back‑and‑forth:
        - Patient: "What days do you have?"
        - AI: "We have Tuesday afternoon or Thursday morning. Which works better?"
        - Patient: "Thursday."
        - AI: flags as "hot reactivation lead" for front desk to finalize and confirm.[^32][^11][^1]
- **Outcome from research:**
    - In the "After AI" day, 15 reactivation messages sent → 3 patients respond → 2 finalized = \$8,400 in previously dormant treatment scheduled.[^1]

**Basic education tracks (start narrow – 1–2 high‑volume treatments):**

- **For each key service (e.g., filler, laser):**
    - Use their existing clinician‑approved instructions and content to create:[^34][^14][^11][^1]
        - **Pre‑visit messages:** expectations, prep checklist, generic downtime bands, FAQs.[^1]
        - **Post‑visit check‑ins:** timed to treatment (24h, 48h, day 3, day 7):[^34][^14][^11][^1]
            - "Hi [Name], you're now on day 3 after [treatment]. Most patients notice [expected symptom]. This is normal. Here's your full instruction PDF: [link]. If you see [red flag symptoms], call us immediately."[^1]
- **AI's role:**
    - Turn dense PDFs and verbal instructions into patient‑friendly, short messages.[^14][^34][^11][^1]
    - Keep language neutral and referential ("as your instructions say")—not inventing new guidance.[^34][^14][^11][^1]

**Real‑world impact (from their pain):**

- **Before:** Patient leaves consult with unscheduled \$5k+ treatment plan; no follow‑up; they never book.[^1]
- **After:** AI reaches out 2 weeks later with friendly reminder; patient books.[^1]
- **Before:** Patient panics 24h post‑filler with normal swelling; calls or walks in unscheduled, creating chaos.[^1]
- **After:** Automated check‑in at 24h normalizes the symptom before panic; unnecessary visits drop dramatically.[^1]

**Pain directly solved:**

- Owner's repeated, failed attempts to "call the \$380k unscheduled list" finally automated.[^1]
- Patients leaving without rebooking ("I'll call back") now get systematic follow‑up instead of being forgotten.[^1]
- Post‑treatment freakouts reduced by proactive education, cutting emergency walk‑ins and after‑hours panic calls.[^1]

**Why it's low‑compliance:**

- All educational content originates from clinic's own clinician‑approved documents and is reviewed/approved before use.[^33][^14][^34][^11][^1]
- AI formats, schedules, and personalizes—but does not invent new medical guidance.[^14][^34][^11][^1]
- Messaging runs through HIPAA‑aware communication tools (Prospyr, Aesthetic Record comms modules, etc.) with consent and opt‑out controls.[^35][^15][^33][^11][^14][^1]
