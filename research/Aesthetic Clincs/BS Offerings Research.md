## 3. Phase 1: 90‑day Patient Flow System (refined with ecosystem insights)

We keep the same three systems, but position them in the language their educators use: **intake, conversion, recall.**

### 3.1 AI Front Desk \& Lead Router

**Positioning (to match Terri/Medspa BizU/Moxie language):**

- “We fix your **intake system** so you stop wasting ad dollars and front desk energy.”[^20]

**Behaviors:**

- Multi‑channel AI receptionist for: phone, web chat, IG DMs.[^11][^13][^1]
- Only handles:
    - Pre‑scripted FAQs (services, pricing bands, downtime ranges, hours, location, insurance position).
    - Intake: name, contact, main concern, timing.
- Feeds into a **single pipeline** with tags and scores (source, intent, likely service, hot/warm/cold).[^12][^13][^11][^1]

**Why it resonates with the Dream‑100 world:**

- Terri Ross and Medspa BizU hammer “speed to lead, answer your phone, script your front desk.”[^20]
- You show:
    - Answer rate → 100%.
    - Response time → <60 seconds.
    - All with their scripts wired into AI.

You are basically “front desk scripting + intake” on autopilot, which is exactly the gap those consultants highlight.[^20]

**Low‑compliance stance unchanged:** non‑clinical FAQs only, PHI stays in EMR/CRM.[^15][^14][^11][^1]

***

### 3.2 Smart Intake \& Provider Briefs

**Positioning (aligned with “CEO mindset” coaches):**

- “We turn your consults into **high‑probability sales conversations** instead of blind first dates.”[^20]

**Behaviors:**

- Short pre‑consult form (goals, timing, downtime, budget band, surgery openness, prior treatments).[^1]
- AI:
    - Scores lead (A/B/C).
    - Produces 1‑page brief: goals, likely fears, context, suggested focus.[^21][^22][^23][^24][^1]

**Why it resonates:**

- Business coaches in aesthetics already teach: “Qualify people before you give up your prime consult slots,” “know their objections before you get in the room.”[^20]
- You are the system that **implements** that, instead of another worksheet or script they never use.

**Low‑compliance stance unchanged:** summarizing patient‑provided info only; no treatment suggestions.[^23][^25][^21][^1]

***

### 3.3 Reactivation \& Education Engine

**Positioning (aligned with Medspa BizU, Ricky Shockley, vendor blogs on recall):**

- “We treat your EMR and CRM like an **ATM** instead of a graveyard of unscheduled treatment and one‑off visits.”[^1][^20]

**Behaviors:**

- Pull unscheduled treatment plans and past visits via EMR API.[^24][^11][^1]
- Segment and send targeted sequences (SMS/email) to:
    - Unfinished treatment plans.
    - Patients due for maintenance (injectables, lasers).
- Use their existing instructions to send pre/post‑treatment messages that reduce anxiety and unnecessary walk‑ins.[^26][^14][^11][^1]

**Why it resonates:**

- AmSpa, Adit, NexHealth, and others already educate on recall, no‑shows, and reactivation as key revenue levers.[^20]
- You come in as: “We wired those best practices into automation that actually runs without another staff member.”

**Low‑compliance stance unchanged:** clinic‑approved content only, via HIPAA‑aware tools; AI formats and schedules.[^25][^27][^14][^15][^11][^1]
