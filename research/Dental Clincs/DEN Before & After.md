## 1. PERSONA PROFILE

**Meet: Dr. Michael Torres, 42**
Owner \& Clinical Lead, Bright Horizon Dental
Location: Frisco, Texas (North Dallas suburbs)
Revenue: \$180k–\$250k/month (depending on case acceptance and hygiene retention)
Staff: 1 Associate Dentist (3 days/week), 3 Hygienists, 2 Dental Assistants, 2 Front Desk (Rachel - lead coordinator, Amy - receptionist), 1 Part-Time Treatment Coordinator (TC)

### Background \& Context

Michael bought the practice 7 years ago from a retiring dentist. He's grown it from \$80k/month to \$180k–\$250k/month by investing heavily in cosmetic dentistry (Invisalign, veneers, implants) and aggressive digital marketing. He's clinically excellent, charismatic with patients, and obsessed with growth—but increasingly aware that **operational chaos is the ceiling**.

**Current Reality:**

- Spending \$10k–\$15k/month on Google PPC and Facebook/Instagram ads targeting Invisalign, dental implants, and "smile makeovers."
- Generating **80–150 new patient leads/month**, but **actual booked new patient exams are only 45–55** (roughly 35–40% conversion).
- Front desk is chronically overwhelmed: insurance verification eats 2–3 hours/day, phones ring constantly during peak times (8–10 AM, 1–3 PM), and voicemail piles up.
- **No-show rate:** 18–22% for new patients, 12–15% for hygiene, 25–30% for high-ticket consults (implants, Invisalign).
- **Recall system:** Mostly manual; hygienists flag overdue patients, front desk is supposed to call them but rarely has time. Thousands of dollars in unscheduled treatment sitting in Dentrix.

**Tools Stack:**

- **Practice Management Software:** Dentrix (scheduling, charting, billing, imaging integration)
- **Phone System:** Weave (VOIP + texting, but underutilized; mostly used for basic reminders)
- **Communication:** Front desk texts patients from Weave portal, but it's clunky; staff also text from personal phones "to be faster"
- **Marketing/Ads:** Managed by external agency (Google, Meta); leads come via phone, web forms, and Facebook lead ads
- **Payments:** Dentrix integrated with CareCredit and practice credit card processor
- **Recall/Reminders:** Dentrix automated postcards + manual calls; Weave sends basic SMS reminders (1–2 days before appt)

**Family \& Obligations:**

- Married, three kids (ages 8, 11, 14)
- Wife is a stay-at-home mom who also manages household logistics; Michael tries to make it to at least 2 of the kids' sports games/week
- Works clinically 4 days/week (Mon–Thu, 8 AM–5:30 PM); Friday mornings for CE, planning, or overflow cases
- Feels constant tension between "being present at home" and "keeping the practice from falling apart"

**Self-Image \& Aspirations:**

- Sees himself as a **clinical leader and entrepreneur**, not "just a dentist"
- Dreams of opening a second location or bringing on another associate to scale production
- Wants to be seen as the "go-to" practice for cosmetic cases and implants in Frisco/Plano area
- Frustrated that he's still "putting out fires" (front desk issues, no-shows, missed leads) instead of focusing on clinical excellence and team development
- Fears being **commoditized** in a market where DSOs and corporate practices are buying up everything

## 4. DECISION POINTS \& FAILURE LOOPS

### **Decision Points (Before AI)**

1. **"Do I hire another front desk person, or just accept we're maxed out?"**
    - Usually: Accept status quo, blame "bad leads"
2. **"Do I spend time calling unscheduled treatment patients myself, or let it sit?"**
    - Usually: Let it sit (too busy clinically)
3. **"Do I increase ad spend, or is it pointless if we can't handle the leads?"**
    - Usually: Paralysis; neither increase nor fix follow-up
4. **"Do I invest in another tool/CRM, or is it just more complexity?"**
    - Usually: Skepticism wins; stays with broken manual processes

### **Decision Points (After AI)**

1. **"Do I trust Douro to handle leads, or review every transcript?"**
    - Initially: Michael reviews obsessively (first week)
    - After 2 weeks: Spot-checks weekly; trusts the system
2. **"Do I let Douro book directly, or require human approval?"**
    - Initially: Human approval required for high-ticket consults
    - After 1 month: Douro books hygiene/exams directly; humans finalize implants/Invisalign
3. **"Do I expand ad spend now that conversion is higher?"**
    - Michael increases budget by 40% because he's confident leads won't be wasted
4. **"Do I hire that second associate, or am I still capacity-constrained?"**
    - With Douro handling intake, Michael realizes capacity is *clinical*, not administrative; hires associate

### **Failure Loops (Before AI)**

- **Loop 1:** Miss calls → Leads lost → Blame "bad leads" or agency → Keep missing calls → Revenue stagnates
- **Loop 2:** Front desk overwhelmed → Skip follow-up/recalls → Revenue leaks → Consider hiring → Worry about overhead → Stay overwhelmed
- **Loop 3:** Spend on ads → Poor conversion → Lower ad spend → Less new patients → Panic, increase ads again → Still poor conversion → Burnout


### **Success Loops (After AI)**

- **Loop 1:** AI responds instantly → More consults booked → Higher revenue → Trust system → Increase ad spend → Even more revenue
- **Loop 2:** No-shows drop → Schedule predictably full → Staff less stressed → Better patient experience → More referrals and 5-star reviews → Organic growth
- **Loop 3:** Unscheduled treatment reactivated → Revenue without ad spend → Confidence in database mining → More systematic reactivation → Continuous production from existing base
