## 1. Clinical \& Practice Management Educators (≈15)

**1.1 Spear Education (Scottsdale, AZ – Workshops, Online, Summits)**
Spear provides advanced restorative/occlusion CE and tightly integrated practice‑management coaching, with workshops, online modules, and study clubs. Owners value Spear for turning complex full‑mouth and interdisciplinary dentistry into reproducible workflows that also drive production and case acceptance. The style is highly structured, evidence‑based, and implementation-focused.[^1][^2]

**1.2 Kois Center (Seattle – Continuum \& Mentorship)**[^3][^1]
Kois is a legendary, data‑heavy CE continuum emphasizing diagnosis, risk assessment, occlusion, and restorative predictability. Dentists pursue Kois for its reputation as a “pinnacle” of comprehensive restorative training and for the halo effect of being a “Kois‑trained dentist.” The tone is rigorous, research‑driven, and clinically obsessive.[^3][^1]

**1.3 The Pankey Institute (Key Biscayne – Continuum + Practice Philosophy)**[^1][^3]
Pankey blends occlusal and restorative mastery with behavioral dentistry, work–life balance, and financial habits. Owners follow Pankey for its emphasis on relationship‑based, fee‑for‑service care and a “whole dentist” philosophy that integrates technical skill, patient communication, and lifestyle design.[^1]

**1.4 The Dawson Academy (Occlusion \& Complete Dentistry, Consulting)**[^4][^1]
Dawson is known for teaching “complete dentistry” based on occlusion, TMJ, and comprehensive exams, and now also offers **Complete Care Consulting** for practice management. Growth‑minded owners like that Dawson explicitly balances clinical excellence with business growth, leadership, and systems.[^4][^1]

**1.5 ACT Dental – Kirk Behrendt (Coaching + CE)**[^5]
ACT Dental coaches practices on leadership, culture, scheduling, and systems, with a strong emphasis on burnout prevention and vision clarity. Owners follow ACT for practical, team-centered systems that improve profitability and quality of life simultaneously. Tone: empathetic, yet demanding about accountability and metrics.[^5]

**1.6 NextLevel Practice – Gary Kadi (Coaching \& Training)**[^6]
NextLevel helps dentists implement a defined business model: documented systems, leadership, team alignment, and financial planning. Owners are drawn to the promise of “love your practice, help your patients, earn more money” without choosing just one. Teaching style is motivational and stepwise, focused on making private practice viable against DSO pressure.[^6]

**1.7 Shared Practices Coaching (Analytics + Systems)**[^7][^8]
Shared Practices blends analytics, ownership strategy, and systems, with coaching aimed at building profitable, simple, sustainable practices. Owners value its data‑driven approach—P\&L analysis, KPI dashboards, call analytics—and concrete playbooks for growth phases, leadership, and delegation.[^9][^7]

**1.8 DoctorsManagement Dental Consulting (Ops + Compliance)**[^10]
DoctorsManagement has a dedicated dental vertical for operations, billing, and compliance consulting. They’re trusted by owners who want a more “traditional” management consultant to streamline workflows, improve profitability, and navigate regulatory complexity.[^10]

**1.9 Levin Group – Roger Levin, DDS (Articles \& Consulting)**[^11]
Roger Levin is one of the longest-running voices in dental practice management, frequently publishing in Dentistry Today and other outlets on economics, new patient flow, case acceptance, and no-shows. Owners see him as a high‑level strategist tying macroeconomic shifts to practical scheduling, marketing, and system design.[^11]

**1.10 McAnally Selling System (Case Acceptance \& High-End Restorative)**[^12]
Mike McAnally critiques the “learn and earn” myth around big-occlusion CE and teaches that advanced restorative work requires equally advanced **promotion and selling systems**. Owners look to him when they’ve invested in Spear/Kois/Pankey but aren’t seeing large-case acceptance financially.[^12]

**1.11 Practice Growth Institute (Dental \& Ortho Coaching, IG)**[^13]
Practice Growth Institute showcases case studies like practices going to \$5M collection and 100+ new patients/month via coaching and systems implementation. Target audience is multi‑doctor, ortho and GP owners who feel they’re doing everything and burning out; the style is highly transformational and leadership-focused.[^13]

**1.12 Ideal Practices (Startup \& Ownership Consulting – via DEO Related Accounts)**[^14]
Ideal Practices is known for guiding scratch start-ups to profitable ownership, with heavy emphasis on demographics, financing, and launch systems. Established owners still follow for positioning, startup-level marketing thinking, and comparisons to acquisition paths.[^14]

**1.13 MGE Management Experts (Classic Production \& Systems Coaching)**[^14]
MGE is a long-standing management and sales training firm in dentistry, focused on communication skills, scheduling, and production efficiency. Many owners credit MGE with teaching them to present comprehensive treatment and keep schedules consistently full.[^14]

**1.14 McAnally Sellability / High-Production Coaching**[^12]
Beyond content, McAnally sells structured programs focused on turning high-fee occlusal/restorative plans into accepted, paid cases. This appeals to owners who already have clinical skills but want to reliably sell \$30–60k+ cases.[^12]

**1.15 Schedule Squad / Other Ops Specialists Featured on Nifty Thrifty**[^15]
Consultants like Schedule Squad appear on Nifty Thrifty Dentists to teach schedule optimization, doctor/hygiene balance, and front-desk load management. Owners follow them for very tactical, low-theory fixes to broken schedules.[^15]

## Synthesis: How This Ecosystem Relates to Douro AI

### What Dental Owners Value Across This Ecosystem

- **Clinical autonomy and excellence**: Spear/Kois/Pankey/Dawson and major CE centers show how seriously owners take high-level dentistry.[^3][^1]
- **Production per hour and profitability**: Business podcasts and coaching (DSI, Bulletproof, Shared Practices, NextLevel, ACT, DEO) continuously hammer on overhead, hygiene productivity, and schedule optimization.[^24][^9][^28][^6][^5][^19]
- **Team leverage and burnout prevention**: Coaching programs and publications emphasize culture, leadership, and burnout-proof operations; front desk overwhelm and staffing are constant topics.[^58][^6][^5][^15]
- **Patient experience and case acceptance**: Bulletproof, Millennial Dentist, Progressive Dental, and case acceptance content emphasize communication, visuals, and follow-up workflows as core business levers.[^23][^11][^44][^40]


### Common Themes in Their Pain-Language

- **Insurance write‑offs \& overhead squeeze**: Publications and business coaches discuss how insurance plus rising costs force owners to work smarter, not just harder.[^11][^38][^6]
- **Staffing shortages \& front desk burnout**: DayDream, Adit, and other vendors document overwhelmed front offices and the scheduling/AR chaos that ensues.[^59][^60][^58]
- **New patient flow vs. conversion**: Marketing articles and podcasts repeatedly point out that ads produce leads, but **phones and follow‑up leak value**—exactly the pain your Phase 1 surfaced.[^61][^19][^15]
- **Case acceptance gaps**: Several podcasts and articles (Bulletproof, Millennial Dentist, McAnally, Progressive) directly address low acceptance as a sales/communication and systems issue, not a purely clinical one.[^23][^44][^12][^40]


### Voices \& Communities Most Aligned with Douro‑Style Lead Handling Automation

Most aligned with Douro’s value proposition (lead → appointment → show):

- **Shared Practices \& Practice by Numbers** – already talking about call analytics, operations AI, and relieving front desk load while increasing performance.[^8][^32]
- **Bulletproof Dental \& DSI** – deep focus on systems, no-shows, and front-desk scripting that you can extend with automation case studies.[^25][^24][^28]
- **DEO Growth Secrets \& Group Dentistry Now** – owners thinking about multi‑site scale are most open to automation around intake and missed call capture.[^19][^41]
- **Nifty Thrifty \& vendor-heavy shows** – explicit episodes on “stop missing calls and losing website leads” and AI support suites. These are natural places to position a HIPAA‑aware, lead‑handling solution.[^15]
- **Levin Group, Dentistry Today, Dental Economics** – data-driven voices discussing no‑shows, case acceptance, and new patient volume as economic indicators offer a perfect framing for your metrics.[^39][^11]


### Credibility Hierarchy

- **Tier 1 (KOLs \& Institutions)**: Spear, Kois, Pankey, Dawson, AmSpa-equivalent dental authorities, Chicago Midwinter, GNYDM – confer technical and professional legitimacy.[^47][^55][^1]
- **Tier 2 (Business/DSO Coaches)**: DSI, DEO, Bulletproof, Shared Practices, NextLevel, ACT – heavily influence how owners think about growth, systems, and technology adoptions.[^27][^9][^6][^5][^19]
- **Tier 3 (Communities \& Facebook Groups)**: Nifty Thrifty, Dental Nachos, Mommy Dentists, Dentaltown – shape vendor reputation and peer validation.[^37][^15][^35][^14]
- **Tier 4 (Micro‑Influencers \& TikTok/IG Dentists)**: The Bentist, Kishen, CG Smile, hygienist creators – drive cultural expectations around what a “modern practice” feels like to patients and younger dentists.[^16][^17]


### Platform Distribution

- **Instagram/TikTok**: Brand, patient‑facing marketing, dentist lifestyle, and micro business education.
- **Podcasts**: Deep strategy and operations—where serious owners go for thinking about systems, KPIs, and leadership.
- **Facebook Groups \& Dentaltown**: Real‑time peer insight, vendor vetting, gossip, and honest reviews.
- **Conferences \& Summits**: Where key tech and automation vendors are normalized, and where owners see real‑world case studies.


### Gaps \& Underserved Segments Relevant to Douro

- **Intake automation framed as an operational discipline**: A lot of content tells dentists to “answer the phone” and “improve case acceptance,” but few treat **lead handling, missed calls, and speed‑to‑lead** as a formal, ownable function with defined KPIs.
- **Front desk–centric AI narratives**: There’s emerging conversation about AI, but very little that speaks concretely to **reducing front desk burnout by offloading repetitive intake and follow‑up tasks**.
- **HIPAA‑aware automation positioning**: Many AI and chat tools are pitched loosely; there is room for an authority voice that connects **compliance, patient experience, and conversion** in one narrative.

Those gaps are where Douro can credibly insert itself—by aligning with the educators and communities above, speaking in their language (production, no‑shows, conversion, team leverage), and anchoring AI in the specific intake pains this ecosystem already acknowledges.
